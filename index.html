Treadmill displays

A treadmill is a machine that can be used to walk or run in place. The goal of our interface is to allow users to easily control the level of their workout by adjusting components such as speed and incline and to monitor the statistics of their workout. Our treadmill uses a touchscreen interface that allows users to control these mechanics. It also has physical knobs that can be used for smaller adjustments and a stand for personal devices to sit on.

Interview & Observation Results

I interviewed 3 college students Sheri, Yevin, and Kelly, and I collected the following results:

- Users typically adjust both speed and incline multiple times in a given workout. This means that they are frequently interacting with the treadmill interface. These adjustments include both small and large jumps which causes users to interact with the interface differently given different circumstances.

- The touchscreen seems to be a popular choice given that users indicated that they tend to jump speeds by large amounts at a time. Because the physical plus/minus knobs only let you adjust by 0.1mph or 1% incline at a time, users indicated that this makes it very difficult to jump from a walking speed to a running speed by using just the knobs.

- Users did not take advantage of the various display options such as watching tv or observing their progress on a track. By observation, I noticed that most people propped their phones onto the interface to watch videos via their cell phone or simply listened to music. A user noted that it is inconvenient to need to log in before being able to use the various applications on the interface, so they stick to their personal device.

- Users kept track of time the most often as a benchmark for their progress, and some users also kept track of distance. The time tracker is in the center of the display which makes it easy to check on, but users indicated that they typicalled used time to measure their cardio workouts either way.

- Users were generally nonchalant about the interface. However, they did note that sometimes the touchscreen buttons were not very responsive which required them to re-enter their speed more than once. They also sometimes found it to be difficult to aim for the right digits when in motion which also causes some difficulties with speed and incline selection. Users indicated that they prefer the knobs when making small changes since they are easier to interact with. However, it was found to be impractical to use these knobs for larger changes, so users leaned toward using the screen in those cases.

Personas

HIIT Haley

Haley is a student who works out casually. She has recently been following a HIIT program that focuses on sprinting for short durations of time. This means she's constantly adjusting her speed by large ranges.

Independent Ira

Ira is a single mom to little Jimmy. She works a full time job and has a very tight schedule between work and taking care of her son. She works remotely from home, so she has a lot of flexibility during the day.

Storyboard - Ira

Ira heads downstairs to the treadmill to get a workout in. She has her headphones plugged in and is ready to go for a quick run on the treadmill!

She steps onto the treadmill and hits go to begin her workout.

Ira sets her phone onto the stand and uses the touchscreen interface to enter in a specific speed and incline. She wants to start off her workout with an inclined walk to warm up.

After a couple minuts, Ira feels warmed up and is ready to start her run. She uses the touchscreen interface to input a specific speed and reset her incline to 0.

Ira runs on the treadmill for 20 minutes while watching a tutorial on how to fix her sink later. She's always multitasking, so she frequently uses the device stand to watch videos while on the treadmill.

Ira adjusts her speed throughout her run. She uses the knobs to adjust the speed 0.1mph at a time during her run to slowly increase her pace.

After she completes her run, she stops the machine and gets ready to pick up her son.
